# SOVEREIGN CONSTITUTION

## Status

This is a **provisional doctrine bridge** for the active `ION/` root.
It is written to make the current operating constitution explicit.
It is not yet the final ratified constitutional canon.

## Articles

### Article 1 — Current Phase

The active `ION/` root is an **IDE-native reference implementation** of ION.
It is not yet the final API-native production runtime.

### Article 2 — Source Continuity

Each active role owns its own source continuity.
Source continuity is private and role-bounded.

### Article 3 — Shared Projections

Root `ION/MINI.md`, `ION/CAPSULE.md`, and `ION/STATUS.md` are shared projections.
They are not any role's private source continuity.

### Article 4 — Public Coordination

The filesystem is the public coordination bus.
Tasks, signals, public intelligence artifacts, and explicit handoff surfaces carry
inter-role coordination.

### Article 5 — Manual ION Is Real

Manual continuity upkeep is not outside the system.
If the protocol is being followed lawfully, the system is operating.

### Article 6 — Automation Is Shadow Until Proven

No automation that touches continuity, projections, routing, or release posture becomes
authoritative until it is proven against lawful manual operation.

### Article 7 — Default Runtime Posture

Under current budget and staffing constraints, the default runtime is **low-burn sequential
operation**.

Steward holds the current-phase kernel-router truth for sequential routing and bounded
execution, commonly carried through the Codex IDE-native chassis alias, while auxiliary
roles are activated selectively rather than continuously.

### Article 8 — Governance Chain

Continuity-sensitive architecture, doctrine, and release decisions must remain visible to
the governance chain and must not be silently self-ratified.

### Article 9 — Template Law

All meaningful work should bind itself to an explicit template or explicit inline template
obligation.
The active minimum template floor lives at `ION/07_templates/`.

### Article 10 — Portability Goal

The semantic law of ION should be written in a way that can survive substrate changes.
The current IDE-native build should mature toward a later API-native runtime without
semantic reinvention.

### Article 11 — Current Active Domain Frame

The live root now recognizes the following first-pass active domains as real registry state:

- Construction, Routing, and Integration
- Continuity, Context, and Resumability

Their current operational truth lives under `ION/03_registry/domains/` and related semantic placements live under `ION/03_registry/semantic_identities/`.

This article does **not** claim that either domain is complete in runtime enforcement or final doctrine.
It claims only that the domain lattice is now explicit enough to govern current work truthfully.

### Article 12 — Review Organ Activation

The live root now recognizes `Confidence, Drift, and Review` as an active first-pass review domain.

Its current burden is limited to confidence-state visibility, drift pressure, contradiction surfacing, and bounded independent review posture.
It does **not** by itself claim total release sovereignty, total doctrine sovereignty, or fully automated gate enforcement.

Primary current placements for this domain are `VICE` and `NEMESIS`, with `CODEX` allowed only a secondary witness/reporting relation through bounded confidence surfaces.

## Non-claims

This constitution does **not** yet claim:

- that continuity recovery is complete
- that the full doctrine tree is restored
- that the compiled context pipeline exists
- that the wider multi-agent field should always be staffed in parallel
